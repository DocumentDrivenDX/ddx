    <title>doc prose: detect definition-by-negation as a structural rule</title>
    <description>
ddx doc prose today catches surface hedge words via contains_any matchers (&#34;robust&#34;, &#34;comprehensive&#34;, &#34;seamless&#34;). It does not catch a more damaging structural defect: defining a subject by what it isn&#39;t.&#xA;&#xA;A real example from a recent HELIX prose pass:&#xA;&#xA;&gt; HELIX models software development as a continuous control loop with seven activities, **not a sequential pipeline**.&#xA;&#xA;The sentence tells the reader what HELIX *is not* (a pipeline) without ever giving the positive shape. The same offense appeared fourteen times across seven documents in different surface forms (&#34;cyclic, not linear&#34;, &#34;loop, not pipeline&#34;, &#34;not sequential&#34;, &#34;not waterfall&#34;). Human review caught it; the prose checker passed it clean.&#xA;&#xA;The defect is structural: a clause whose main predicate is `not &lt;noun-phrase&gt;` with no positive predicate in the same sentence describing what the subject *is*. Prose like that reads as hedging and forces the reader to infer what the author meant.&#xA;&#xA;Proposed rule shape:&#xA;&#xA;Add a new kind: negation_predicate to the ruleSpec schema (cli/internal/docprose/assets.go). Implementation in the embedded checker (cli/internal/docprose/checker.go) scans sentences for the pattern:&#xA;&#xA;  &lt;subject&gt; [is|are|stays|remains] not &lt;noun-phrase|adjective&gt;&#xA;&#xA;Fires only when no positive predicate appears in the same sentence describing the subject. A positive predicate is anything matching `&lt;subject&gt; [is|are|...] &lt;noun-phrase|adjective&gt;` *without* the `not`.&#xA;&#xA;Default rule in the planning pack:&#xA;&#xA;  - id: prose.definition_by_negation&#xA;    severity: warning&#xA;    kind: negation_predicate&#xA;    rationale: This sentence defines its subject by what it is not. Readers infer what you mean; stronger prose names the positive shape directly.&#xA;    suggested_edit: Replace the negation with a sentence whose main predicate describes what the subject is. Keep the negation only if a positive predicate follows in the same paragraph.&#xA;&#xA;Scope notes — legitimate uses of negation should not fire:&#xA;- Sections explicitly enumerating what something is not (&#34;What HELIX Is Not&#34;, &#34;Non-Goals&#34;, &#34;Out of Scope&#34;, &#34;Anti-Patterns&#34;).&#xA;- Contrastive constructions where the positive follows immediately (&#34;HELIX is not a CLI; it is a methodology&#34;) — the second clause supplies the positive predicate.&#xA;&#xA;The rule needs section-context awareness: ignore matches inside H2/H3 sections whose heading matches `What .+ Is Not|Non-?Goals?|Out of Scope|Anti-Patterns?`. This is the first prose rule that needs heading context, so it likely requires a small extension to the checker per-rule context model.&#xA;&#xA;Out of scope:&#xA;- AI-grade semantic detection. The heuristic above catches the structural defect; deeper analysis is a separate request.&#xA;- Cross-paragraph context. Start with same-sentence detection; widen if false-negative rate is high in practice.
    </description>
