    <title>execute-bead: escalate power on no_changes/no_evidence outcomes instead of re-attempting at the same tier</title>
    <description>
PROBLEM: When an execute-bead attempt ends in no_changes / no_evidence_produced (notably the degenerate case &#34;agent exited without a commit or no_changes_rationale.txt&#34;), the worker unclaims and leaves the bead OPEN at the SAME power tier — it never escalates to a stronger model. Observed live: fizeau-d31ff685 (a feasible P1 bead whose helper functions all exist) failed TWICE, both attempts routed to claude/sonnet (power 8), and the bead was just left open for an operator to notice. A turn that burns the model yet lands ZERO committable evidence is the strongest signal the tier is too weak — that is exactly when the ladder must climb, not shrug and re-attempt at the same power.&#xA;&#xA;ROOT CAUSE: adjudicateNoChangesContract (cli/internal/agent/try/no_changes.go:217-289) maps no_changes/bad-attempt outcomes ONLY to LIFECYCLE actions (keep_open_smart_retry, operator_required, bad_attempt_no_cooldown, retry_later_cooldown) — there is NO power-escalation action in that path. IncrNoChangesCount (cli/internal/agent/try/attempt.go:300) tracks the per-bead count but only feeds the satisfaction check, not the next attempt&#39;s power floor. The EscalationCount field (cli/internal/agent/execute_bead_loop.go:598) is serialized but never raises the execute attempt&#39;s MinPower. The escalation ladder (internal/escalation, nextEscalatedPowerClass at execute_bead_post_review.go:711) is wired for REVIEW/hard-failure escalation, not for no-evidence execute outcomes.&#xA;&#xA;PROPOSED FIX: On a no_changes/no_evidence bad-attempt outcome, escalate the NEXT execute attempt&#39;s power floor (via the escalation package / nextEscalatedPowerClass), keyed on IncrNoChangesCount, so the bead climbs power 8 -&gt; 9 -&gt; 10 across attempts rather than repeating at the same tier. The degenerate &#34;no commit AND no rationale&#34; case (no_evidence_produced) should escalate IMMEDIATELY (strongest weak-tier signal); a clean no_changes-with-rationale that fails the satisfaction check should escalate after the existing threshold. Bound by the ladder cap (max power / ladder-exhaustion) so it does not climb forever; on exhaustion at top power, fall through to the existing operator_required/park path (that is the legitimate escalate-to-operator point, not a fresh open bead at power 8).&#xA;&#xA;NON-SCOPE: changing the satisfaction-check (already_satisfied) path; review-time escalation (already implemented); cost-cap policy.
