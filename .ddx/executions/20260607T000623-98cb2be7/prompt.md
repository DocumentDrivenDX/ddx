    <description>
PROBLEM: A peer worker reclaims a bead whose claim lease is merely AGED, even when the owning process is still alive — a primary cause of parallel execution (see ddx-d740539d, two attempts overlapping 16.6 min).&#xA;&#xA;ROOT CAUSE: claimLeaseIsStale (cli/internal/bead/store.go:1014-1025) and ClaimHeartbeatFresh (cli/internal/bead/claim_liveness.go:138-150) decide reclaimability by AGE ALONE (time.Since(rec.UpdatedAt) &lt;= HeartbeatTTL), even though ClaimLeaseRecord records PID and Machine (claim_liveness.go:23, written at store.go:928/:977). Takeover never consults them. By contrast the tracker DIR-lock correctly uses positive death: breakStaleLockDir -&gt; processAlive(pid) (cli/internal/bead/lock.go:88-98). The claim lease has the PID but ignores it, so an aged-but-alive same-machine owner is treated as dead and reclaimed.&#xA;&#xA;PROPOSED FIX: In claimLeaseIsStale, when the lease is older than HeartbeatTTL BUT lease.Machine == this host AND processAlive(rec.PID) is true, treat it as NOT stale (owner alive — refuse takeover) up to a hard fallback ceiling (&gt;= the max lock-hold cap). When Machine differs (PID unknowable cross-machine) or PID is dead, fall back to the existing age-based staleness. Reuse the existing processAlive primitive (lock.go); keep the legacy heartbeatIsStale fallback for rows with no sidecar.&#xA;&#xA;NON-SCOPE: the intake heartbeat gap (sibling bead C1 — they are defense-in-depth; both close the window); land-lock holds.
