    <description>
PROBLEM&#xA;When `ddx work --watch` cannot make progress on a full queue, it looks healthy from the outside — it emits loop.idle every idle-interval and keeps polling — but claims nothing. In the parent incident (ddx-f9ddaa68), 23 ready beads sat unclaimed for 30+ minutes while the worker logged loop.idle on a ~50s cadence. The operator had no top-level signal that claim throughput had collapsed; silent idle on a full queue is a stuck state masquerading as healthy.&#xA;&#xA;ROOT CAUSE&#xA;The drain loop tracks consecutive idle cycles on an identical pre-claim blocker (preClaimIdleEscalationThreshold=5, cli/internal/agent/execute_bead_loop.go:3314, escalated at cli/internal/agent/execute_bead_loop.go:1338-1349, ddx-df77e668) but has no notion of claim SUCCESS RATE across a rolling window of attempts. A loop that claims beads which then fail (no_changes/error), or that skips for varied reasons that never repeat the same blocker detail, never trips the same-blocker idle escalation and produces no operator-visible throughput warning. The `ddx work` flag surface (cli/cmd/work.go:88-89) exposes only --watch and --idle-interval. Post-hoc analysis exists (cli/cmd/work_analyze.go) but computes reviewer FP/FN rates from logs, not a live claim-success rate.&#xA;&#xA;PROPOSED FIX&#xA;1. Track a rolling window of the last N claim attempts (default 10) in the drain loop (cli/internal/agent/execute_bead_loop.go), recording for each whether it produced a successful claim+attempt vs a skip/claim-failure.&#xA;2. When the success rate over a full window drops below a configurable threshold (default 0.0, i.e. a full window of non-successes), emit one operator-visible stderr warning to runtime.Log and one structured loop event (reusing the operator-attention/loop event path next to the idle escalation at cli/internal/agent/execute_bead_loop.go:1338-1349) per below-threshold crossing — not every idle cycle. A subsequent success clears the warned state.&#xA;3. Add flags --claim-rate-window (default 10) and --claim-rate-threshold (default 0.0) to cli/cmd/work.go, threaded into the loop runtime.&#xA;&#xA;NON-SCOPE&#xA;Do NOT remove or alter the existing same-blocker idle escalation (preClaimIdleEscalationThreshold, ddx-df77e668) — this is a complementary signal, not a replacement.
