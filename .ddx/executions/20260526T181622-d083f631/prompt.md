PROBLEM&#xA;ddx work --watch can claim a bead, fail to resolve a route, and hang in the route-resolution step indefinitely. Heartbeats keep emitting (phase=running, last_activity_at advancing every 30s) but harness/model/route fields stay empty and no LLM call ever happens. Strongly suggests the same wedge: route resolution returns &#39;no route&#39; but the worker treats it as &#39;wait forever&#39; instead of &#39;release bead with error&#39;.&#xA;&#xA;WHY THIS IS A BUG&#xA;1. Lease without progress: holding a lease for 80+ minutes with no LLM call, no commit, no resolved route is indistinguishable from a crashed worker, but heartbeats keep ticking so the staleness reaper (if any) never fires.&#xA;2. Single-worker starvation: 30 ready beads, one stuck worker, queue stops advancing.&#xA;3. State inconsistency: bead has a claim but bead status reports 0 in_progress, suggesting the claim and the lifecycle status are not atomically coupled.&#xA;4. No actionable error surface: the operator only learns about the wedge by tailing agent-logs and noticing the empty harness/route fields. The watch loop&#39;s stdout shows nothing because heartbeats are JSONL-only.&#xA;5. The harness for execute-bead inherits zero feedback when route resolution silently fails — even when a route eventually resolves, the wedge has stolen 80 minutes of throughput.&#xA;&#xA;PROPOSED FIX&#xA;A. Route-resolution timeout: the resolution step must be bounded by a configurable timeout (default 60s). On timeout: release the lease with a structured error, mark the attempt as failed, do not retry without operator attention.&#xA;B. Progress timeout independent of heartbeats: a watchdog that fires when last_activity_at has not advanced beyond a phase-specific budget (e.g., 5 minutes in &#39;resolving&#39;, 30 minutes in &#39;running&#39; with a harness/model set, etc.) — phase-empty heartbeats with no real work are the failure signature.&#xA;C. Lease+lifecycle atomicity: claiming a bead must atomically set status to in_progress; releasing on failure must atomically clear the claim. ddx bead status counts must reflect claimed beads.&#xA;D. Operator-visible error surface: when a worker releases a lease due to timeout or wedge detection, emit a top-level operator-attention event with bead-id, attempt-id, last-activity, and a short diagnosis. ddx bead operator-attention should surface it.&#xA;E. Worker exit on repeated wedge: if the same bead wedges twice in a row, the worker should exit (or stop claiming that bead) rather than keep eating the queue.&#xA;&#xA;CROSS-REF&#xA;Filed from Fizeau session 2026-05-17/18 during the claude-tui chain rollout.
