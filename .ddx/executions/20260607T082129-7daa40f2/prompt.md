    <description>
**Problem**&#xA;&#xA;When `ddx work --watch` runs against a `ddx server` that is partially&#xA;unavailable (e.g. TLS handshake failure, agent-service connection&#xA;refusal, persistent route-resolution failures with no candidates), the&#xA;worker treats every individual bead as the failure unit. Each attempt&#xA;gets flagged with `operator_attention` via the progress-watchdog or&#xA;`execution_failed` via the routing preflight, then the next bead is&#xA;tried, then the next.&#xA;&#xA;Result: a single server-side problem (one bad TLS cert, an expired&#xA;provider credential, a stale model catalog) silently turns the entire&#xA;ready queue into &#34;needs human&#34; beads, even though the *beads* are fine&#xA;— the *server* is broken. The worker keeps idling, the operator sees&#xA;&#34;0 closures in 24h&#34; with no clear signal that this is environmental.&#xA;&#xA;Observed concretely on niflheim on 2026-05-31:&#xA;&#xA;- `ddx server` was serving a TLS cert from&#xA;  `$DDX_PROJECT_ROOT/.ddx/server/tls/cert.pem` (Apr 17 fingerprint)&#xA;  while the client `ddx work` pinned the cert in `~/.ddx/server/tls/`&#xA;  (May 18 fingerprint). The server logged&#xA;  `http: TLS handshake error from 127.0.0.1:XXXXX: remote error: tls:&#xA;  bad certificate` every ~5 min for ~36 hours.&#xA;- Every `niflheim-71365a9f` attempt during that window failed with&#xA;  `ResolveRoute: no viable routing candidate: 16 candidates rejected,&#xA;  outcome_reason=no_viable_provider`.&#xA;- After 15+ attempts the worker flagged the bead for operator&#xA;  attention, then quietly idled with `execution-ready=0 blocked=47&#xA;  operator-attention=17 needs-human/investigation=10`.
