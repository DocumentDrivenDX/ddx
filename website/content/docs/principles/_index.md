The ten domain principles that shape DDx as a document-driven software
factory. Each page expands the principle, summarizes the evidence behind
it, and names the DDx mechanism that enacts it.

The principles are grounded in research syntheses RSCH-001 through
RSCH-010 in `docs/helix/00-discover/research/` and the references they
cite in `docs/helix/00-discover/references/`.

## The ten

1. [Spec-first development](spec-first-development/) — specs, not code, are the durable artifact.
2. [Executable specifications](executable-specifications/) — a spec that cannot be checked is a wish.
3. [Audit trail required](audit-trail-required/) — every artifact traces to the inputs that caused it.
4. [Context is king](context-is-king/) — the brief is the lever on quality and cost.
5. [Work is a DAG](work-is-a-dag/) — dependencies are explicit edges, not prose.
6. [Right-size the model](right-size-the-model/) — cheapest tool that does the job; escalate on failure.
7. [Avoid vendor lock-in](avoid-vendor-lock-in/) — standardize the contract, swap the implementation.
8. [Drift is debt](drift-is-debt/) — divergence between spec, code, and config compounds.
9. [Least privilege for agents](least-privilege-for-agents/) — scope file and tool surface to the task.
10. [Inspect and adapt](inspect-and-adapt/) — evidence-bound generate-then-review loops, not generic retros.

For the canonical short form, see `docs/helix/01-frame/principles.md` in
the repository.
