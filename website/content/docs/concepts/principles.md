The load-bearing principles behind DDx now live under
[/docs/principles/](/docs/principles/). Each has its own page expanding
the principle, the evidence behind it, and the DDx mechanism that enacts it.

## The ten principles

1. [Spec-first development](/docs/principles/spec-first-development/) — specs, not code, are the durable artifact.
2. [Executable specifications](/docs/principles/executable-specifications/) — a spec that cannot be checked is a wish.
3. [Audit trail required](/docs/principles/audit-trail-required/) — every artifact traces to the inputs that caused it.
4. [Context is king](/docs/principles/context-is-king/) — the brief is the lever on quality and cost.
5. [Work is a DAG](/docs/principles/work-is-a-dag/) — dependencies are explicit edges, not prose.
6. [Right-size the model](/docs/principles/right-size-the-model/) — cheapest tool that does the job; escalate on failure.
7. [Avoid vendor lock-in](/docs/principles/avoid-vendor-lock-in/) — standardize the contract, swap the implementation.
8. [Drift is debt](/docs/principles/drift-is-debt/) — divergence between spec, code, and config compounds.
9. [Least privilege for agents](/docs/principles/least-privilege-for-agents/) — scope file and tool surface to the task.
10. [Inspect and adapt](/docs/principles/inspect-and-adapt/) — evidence-bound generate-then-review loops, not generic retros.

For the canonical short form, see `docs/helix/01-frame/principles.md` in the repository.
